What Predicts Stability?
Higher Baseline Volume

Clinicians serving >100 Medicaid patients initially were more likely to maintain stable participation

Community Health Centers

Affiliation with CHCs associated with greater stability and higher odds of increases

Practice Setting

Rural practice settings linked to higher odds of major decreases in enrollee volume

Gender Differences

Varied patterns by specialty, with female clinicians showing different participation trends

Policy Implications
  • Access to care may be more volatile than previously understood
  • Need for policies supporting consistent clinician engagement with Medicaid
  • Community Health Centers play crucial role in maintaining stable networks
  • Rural areas require targeted workforce retention strategies
  • Tracking participation trends critical as Medicaid faces potential funding changes
  • Managed care contracting should prioritize "stable participant" clinicians
Methodology: Longitudinal analysis using T-MSIS Analytic Files tracking changes in unique Medicaid enrollees served annually. Major changes defined as >90% increase or decrease from baseline.
Authors: Mandar Bodas, Qian Luo, Anushree Vichare
